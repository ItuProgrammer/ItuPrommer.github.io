    Breast cancer is the most common cancer among women in South Africa,
    with rates increasing over recent years.Here are key insights on breast 
    cancer in South Africa:

    
  
        Malithabo ?Prevalence and Mortality:

    Breast cancer accounts for about 25% of all cancer diagnoses in South 
    African women.The incidence rate is around 43 per 100,000 women, higher 
    in urban than rural areas.Women are generally diagnosed between ages 50-55, 
    younger than in many high-income countries.High mortality rates are linked to 
    late-stage diagnoses and limited healthcare access, with around 4,000 deaths in 2020.
    
  Early Detection and Screening:

    Access to mammography is limited, especially in rural areas.Public health recommends screening
    for women over 40, but few have access.Only 30% of cases are detected early,hindered by 
    low awareness and limited screening programs.
    
  Survival Rates:

    Late detection leads to lower survival rates, with only 20-30% surviving late-stage diagnoses 
    versus 80-90% for early detection.Black women tend to be diagnosed at later stages and have
    worse outcomes than white women, influenced by socio-economic factors.
